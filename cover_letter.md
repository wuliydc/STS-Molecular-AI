Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"RNA-NGS Substantially Extends the Diagnostic Reach of FISH in Soft Tissue Sarcoma: An AI-Integrated Real-World Cohort Study of 1,489 Patients"** for consideration as a Research Article in *Nature Communications*.

**Why this work is important**

Soft tissue sarcomas (STS) are rare but biologically heterogeneous malignancies with more than 70 histological subtypes. Accurate molecular subtyping is essential for prognosis and targeted therapy selection, yet the optimal integration of the three principal molecular testing modalities — fluorescence in situ hybridisation (FISH), RNA-based next-generation sequencing (RNA-NGS), and DNA-NGS — remains poorly defined in real-world clinical practice.

**What we did and found**

We assembled the largest real-world multi-modal molecular testing cohort in STS to date (12,385 records, 1,489 patients, single tertiary centre, 2018–2025) and addressed four previously unanswered questions:

1. **Incremental diagnostic yield:** RNA-NGS identified clinically actionable fusion genes in 28.3% of FISH-negative patients — a substantially higher incremental yield than previously reported in smaller series. DNA-NGS revealed actionable therapeutic targets (NTRK, ALK, BRAF, TMB-H, MSI-H) in 15.7% of patients tested by all three methods.

2. **FISH–NGS discordance:** The overall FISH–RNA-NGS discordance rate was 33.7% among 1,052 paired cases. We systematically classified discordance into biologically distinct subtypes (Type A: FISH+/RNA−; Type B: FISH−/RNA+), characterised their clinical correlates, and propose a practical management algorithm for pathologists. A discordance prediction model achieved AUC=0.839.

3. **AI diagnostic classifier:** A multi-modal AI classifier integrating all three modalities achieved macro-AUC=0.780 across 15 tumour subtypes, with SHAP-based feature attribution providing interpretable molecular reasoning for each prediction. Ablation experiments quantified the independent contribution of each modality.

4. **Clinical translation:** We developed STS-Molecular-AI, an open-source clinical decision support tool (FastAPI backend + Gradio web interface), freely available at https://github.com/wuliydc/STS-Molecular-AI and https://huggingface.co/spaces/liyuanys/STS-Molecular-AI, enabling real-time integration of molecular inputs into diagnostic and therapeutic recommendations.

**Why *Nature Communications***

This work sits at the intersection of molecular pathology, clinical oncology, and artificial intelligence — an audience directly served by *Nature Communications*. The study addresses a high-impact clinical problem (STS affects ~1% of adult cancers but carries disproportionate diagnostic complexity), provides a large real-world dataset and open-source tools directly applicable to other centres worldwide, and delivers findings with immediate practical implications for molecular testing strategy.
